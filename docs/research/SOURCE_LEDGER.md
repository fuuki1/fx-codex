The current COT feature needs an additional semantic warning. It subtracts net-position/open-interest ratios across different contracts. The USD leg is ICE U.S. Dollar Index futures, while the other configured legs are individual currency futures. Those contracts have different baskets, quote conventions, participant mixes and open-interest denominators. Normalising each by its own open interest does not make the legs symmetric. The result must be named a **cross-contract public positioning proxy**, not USD customer flow, consolidated FX positioning or a directly comparable pair exposure.

## Known source gaps

- A common PIT envelope exists as a research utility, but the macro, COT, calendar, news and backtest loaders above are not yet routed through a common immutable ingestion store. Its presence must not be described as system-wide PIT enforcement.
- Dukascopy/JForex historical-data pages were identified as a potential market-data source, but no stable, versioned public API contract was accepted in this audit. It remains **candidate only**, not a production dependency.
- Stooq, FairEconomy, FXStreet RSS, Google News RSS and the community TradingView scanner do not currently have an accepted source contract covering source timestamps, corrections, first-seen evidence, licensing/redistribution and immutable replay. Their current use remains research-only.
- The FairEconomy archive is consumed as a local as-of revision history only when the full PIT revision contract is present. Stable source identity, authoritative cancellation/revision semantics, actual/revised values and observations predating local capture remain unavailable; heuristic-identity runs are promotion-ineligible.
- No public source reviewed here supplies this repository with bank dealer customer flow, ECN depth, prime-broker flow, or complete OTC market data. Candle/tick-volume features must be named proxies.
